Biography

Soprano Angela Yam is a performer whose repertoire ranges from opera to oratorio to avant-garde chamber music. Angela Yam joins Fargo-Moorhead Opera as a 2022 Gate City Bank Young Artist, singing the titular role in Mozart’s Bastien and Bastienne, Clorinda in Rossini’s La Cenerentola, and Jazz Trio in Bernstein’s Trouble in Tahiti. In May 2022, she joins Opera Saratoga as a Festival Artist, singing Johanna in Sweeney Todd and the soprano soloist in Rossini’s Petite messe solennelle.

Angela joined Santa Fe Opera as an Apprentice Singer in 2021, performing Cobweb in A Midsummer Night's Dream and covering Agave in John Corigliano’s Lord of Cries (world premiere), and singing in the chorus of Tchaikovsky’s Eugene Onegin and Mozart’s Le nozze di Figaro. In Fall 2021, Angela premiered the roles of Katerina in The Glassblower's Daughter (Koning and Artinian) with Lightning House Players and The Bride in The Bedbug (Asher) while a Resident Artist with Promenade Opera Project.

Angela has appeared as Mary Crawford in Dove’s Mansfield Park with Opera Modesto and as the soprano soloist for Bach’s Magnificat and Handel’s Messiah with Camerata California. While completing her Graduate Diploma (2021) from the New England Conservatory, Angela performed as Blanche de la Force (Poulenc: Dialogues of the Carmelites), the Coloratura Soprano (Argento: Postcard from Morocco), Calisto (Cavalli: La Calisto), and the soprano soloist in Haydn’s Die Schöpfung. In her spare time, she enjoys roller-skating and playing the baritone saxophone.
